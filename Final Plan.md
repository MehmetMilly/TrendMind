Implement a full front-end rebuild of TrendMind’s workspace based on the TrendMind 2.0 idea.



This brief overrides earlier workspace directions if there is any conflict.



Important context:

I am not optimizing first for “real enterprise work density” or ultra-practical dashboard behavior.

I am optimizing for a front-end that looks genuinely great, feels premium and intentional, and impresses judges and viewers immediately.

It should still feel coherent and product-like, not random or flashy, but visual impact matters more than operational realism.



The current version has a major problem:

it is far too text-heavy.

It feels like a beautifully styled document or dossier instead of a striking product workspace.

There are too many labels, too many explanatory lines, too many cards that rely on reading instead of visual hierarchy and composition.



That needs to change.



What to keep from the current direction:

\- the general premium visual DNA

\- the warm ivory / gold / forest-green palette family

\- the sense that this is a serious, refined AI campaign product

\- the staged campaign structure:

&#x20; Brief -> Research -> Strategy -> Draft -> Trial -> Studio -> Launch



What to stop doing:

\- do not treat the workspace like a long text document

\- do not make the product feel like a Notion page with prettier cards

\- do not fill every phase with paragraphs, taglines, labels, and helper copy

\- do not let copy do the job that layout, contrast, spacing, grouping, and scale should do

\- do not create a cluttered “AI dashboard” with too many permanent utility panels

\- do not preserve the current layouts out of loyalty



Core design direction:

TrendMind should feel like a premium AI campaign studio.

The campaign is still one coherent journey, but the workspace should present it as a sequence of visually distinct stage-like experiences rather than one long readable artifact.



In other words:

the product should feel continuous,

but it should not feel like a literal document.



Main structural direction:



1\. Permanent left sidebar

Keep a permanent left sidebar.

It should be slimmer than the old one, visually strong, and part of the product identity.

Think confident, elegant, and compact — not bulky.

It should contain the workspace navigation, campaign threads, and user area.

This is one place where permanence is good, because it makes the product feel grounded and designed.



2\. Compact top shell

Keep the top strip compact.

It should hold the campaign title, live status, and a few high-level actions like share/export.

No wasted vertical space.

No oversized decorative header behavior.



3\. Phase navigation stays visible

Keep a phase tab/ribbon row near the top:

Brief, Research, Strategy, Draft, Trial, Studio, Launch

These phases should always be visible and easy to jump between.



But:

they should not feel like a locked wizard,

and the workspace should not show all phases with equal visual weight at once.



4\. The center canvas must dominate

The center of the screen is where the product must win.

It needs to be much more visual, much less verbose, and much more composed.

The active phase should own the viewport.

Other phases can exist as nearby previews, anchors, or condensed states, but the current “everything turns into stacked beige reading sections” direction should be abandoned.



5\. No permanent right-side operational panel

Do not bring back a large permanent right rail.

That narrows the canvas and makes the product feel busier and more tool-like in the wrong way.

If deeper agent/context information is needed, use an on-demand drawer, peek panel, or contextual overlay.

The default state should protect the center canvas.



High-level workspace philosophy:

This is not an internal admin tool.

This is not a dashboard-first product.

This is not a documentation page.



This should feel closer to:

a creative studio,

a campaign lab,

a polished interactive presentation,

and a premium AI-native product.



The product should feel “showable.”

A judge should be able to glance at it and immediately feel that it is special.



Visual direction:

Use a refined workspace direction as the base, but allow controlled cinematic energy in selected moments.



That means:

\- calm, elegant, premium overall shell

\- stronger visual contrast than the current version

\- more decisive composition

\- more object-led surfaces

\- less paragraph copy

\- more hero moments

\- more visual rhythm between phases



Use the existing general palette DNA, but make it feel deeper and more intentional.

The workspace should not read as flat beige with text blocks.

Introduce richer contrast between calm phases and feature phases.



Typography:

\- Serif should be used strategically for hero copy, campaign copy, key quotes, and high-emphasis moments.

\- Sans should handle structure, navigation, metadata, and utility.

\- Reduce explanatory subtitle text aggressively.

\- Default body copy should be shorter, tighter, and quieter.

\- Make important lines bigger and more memorable.

\- Stop letting every section become a reading exercise.



Content compression rules:

This is extremely important.



In the normal default view:

\- no paragraph should feel like it needs actual “reading effort”

\- most cards should communicate in one glance

\- supporting copy should usually be one short sentence max

\- metadata should collapse into chips, bars, scores, icons, or one-line rows

\- long rationale should live behind inspect/expand behavior, not always on screen

\- section subtitles should be very short or removed entirely

\- if a block feels like a mini article, redesign it



Phase-by-phase direction:



Brief:

Do not present Brief like a long campaign intake form or dossier.

Make it feel like a brand board / campaign setup surface.

It should quickly communicate the campaign thesis, brand, audience, tone, and constraints in a highly legible and visually elegant way.

Use concise rows, chips, compact field groups, and one strong central summary moment.

It should feel like “the campaign has been set,” not “here is a lot of paperwork.”



Research:

Research should feel like a signal board, not a wall of analysis.

Use a mosaic / grid of strong insight cards.

Each card should have:

\- a clear type

\- one strong insight headline

\- one supporting line at most

\- a confidence or strength signal

\- quiet source attribution

Make the section feel analytical and alive, but never verbose.



Strategy:

This phase should feel like three strategic posters or three angle objects.

The three angles must be visually distinct and instantly understandable.

Each one should have:

\- a strong name

\- one hero line

\- a few supporting points or proof chips

\- one risk note

\- a clear commit state

This section should feel sharp, editorial, and comparative.

Not essay-like.



Draft:

This should feel like a composition surface, not three long copy cards.

Show the draft pieces as a kit:

hooks, bodies, CTAs, maybe supporting atoms.

Then show the composed variants below in a clearer, more presentational way.

The key is that the variants should feel punchy and inspectable.

Critique should not appear as full text notes everywhere.

Compress critique into short chips, short comments, or expandable annotations.



Trial:

This must be the visual centerpiece.

It should feel like the campaign enters a testing chamber or stage.

Make it darker, more focused, and more dramatic than the rest of the app.

This is where the audience simulation comes alive.

The active variant should feel centered and important.

Personas and reactions should feel theatrical but controlled.

This is the place where the app proves it is not just “AI writes ad copy.”

This phase should be memorable in screenshots and in live demo.



Studio:

This should feel like a real creative surface.

It needs a large visual preview/artboard.

It should look meaningfully more visual than the current implementation.

Do not let it feel like just another content card next to a layer list.

The composition needs to feel intentional, polished, and studio-like.

Keep controls minimal in the default view.

Let the artwork carry more of the surface.



Launch:

This should feel like the payoff.

A premium showcase of the final campaign.

The winning variant should feel elevated and presentation-ready.

Platform adaptations should look like designed outputs, not placeholders in boxes.

Alternates and risk notes should exist, but in a compact and elegant form.

This phase should feel like “ship-ready campaign deck,” not “last section of a document.”



Interaction design:

\- Every important element should respond.

\- Selections should reveal depth on demand, not dump everything on screen by default.

\- Hover, focus, peek, and expand behaviors should do more work.

\- The product should feel alive through subtle motion, phase changes, emphasis shifts, and controlled transitions.

\- Avoid noisy constant motion.

\- Motion should support elegance and stagecraft, especially in Trial and Studio.



Agent presence:

AI should feel present, but not through a giant always-open operations panel.

Use lighter, better tools:

\- subtle live status in the shell

\- compact agent attribution where relevant

\- optional on-demand agent drawer or command panel

\- contextual insight when selecting something

The product should not feel like six bots are constantly talking at the user.



Judge-first mindset:

I want the app to impress judges and viewers.

So optimize for:

\- visual clarity in 3 seconds

\- strong screenshots

\- memorable phase transitions

\- one or two real “wow” moments

\- a polished, premium, modern feel

\- distinct personality without becoming chaotic



This does not mean making it flashy everywhere.

It means the product should feel curated and deliberately staged.



Specific anti-goals:

\- no long beige reading surfaces

\- no section that is mostly labels and text

\- no “beautiful CRM” feeling

\- no reintroduction of a huge permanent right-side rail

\- no treating all phases with the same visual energy

\- no flattening everything into cards with similar density

\- no timid hierarchy



Implementation priority order:

1\. Rebuild the shell and overall layout system.

2\. Rebuild the phase presentation model so the active phase dominates.

3\. Aggressively reduce text and compress metadata throughout.

4\. Redesign all seven phases with distinct visual character.

5\. Make Trial the showpiece and Studio the second visual peak.

6\. Add interaction polish, contextual depth, and motion refinement.

7\. Final pass on spacing, hierarchy, and premium finish.



Acceptance criteria:

When this is done, the workspace should:

\- feel significantly less text-heavy than the current version

\- have a permanent left sidebar

\- keep the center canvas visually dominant

\- feel more like a premium AI campaign studio than a document

\- have clearly distinct phase personalities

\- look impressive in screenshots

\- have a standout Trial phase

\- feel coherent, restrained, and high quality rather than random or flashy



Before coding, think carefully about the product composition and hierarchy.

Do not just restyle the current screens.

This should be a real front-end redesign and rebuild.
